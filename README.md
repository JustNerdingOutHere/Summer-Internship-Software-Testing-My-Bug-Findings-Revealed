# Summer-Internship-Software-Testing-My-Bug-Findings-Revealed

Hello, let me guide you through my IT internship project. During my summer internship at a technical sales company (more in my CV), I reviewed their website and their partner website using exploratory white-box manual QA methods, documenting bugs via screenshots and DevTools code. The bugs found are ranging from broken UI to  high-severity systemic functional ones. To make it comprehensible, commentaries were added to each of the screenshots. Feel free to browse through those. I believe that practical experience here taught me the most indeed.

Note: To protect the company's privacy, manual test cases aren't being published here due to containing sensitive information that could lead to misuse (links leading to a real website I anonymized). However, complete test documentation will be available upon a request for companies interested in reviewing my methodology. The names of the screenshot pictures also serve as a brief explanation of what's wrong with the website design/functioning.

For better orientation, I recommend to browse those screenshot snaps starting from V1-V13, then switching to T1-T3 as those letters are just markers of two different websites (created by the same developer(s) though).
